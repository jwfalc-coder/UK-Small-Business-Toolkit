The HMRC late payment interest rate is the Bank of England base rate plus 2.5%, currently 7.25% per annum (base rate 4.75%). This rate is updated by HMRC when the base rate changes.

Other Companies House consequences

Late filing is not just a financial penalty. Persistent late filing can result in the company being struck off the register, which dissolves the company and transfers its assets to the Crown. Directors can also be disqualified for up to 15 years for persistent failure to file on time.

CT late payment rate

HMRC charges interest at the Bank of England base rate plus 2.5% on overdue corporation tax. The rate is currently 7.25% per annum. It is compounded daily and starts accruing from the day after the payment deadline.

Avoiding penalties

Use the Companies House deadline tracker to set reminders. If you know you will miss a deadline, you can apply for a deadline extension in advance (allowed in genuine exceptional circumstances only).
