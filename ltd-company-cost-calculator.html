Sole trader comparison: A sole trader pays no Companies House fees, no employer NI, and has much lower accountancy costs (typically £300 to £600/year). The additional overhead of ltd company status needs to be justified by the tax savings from incorporating. Use the Sole Trader vs Ltd Company Calculator to find your break-even profit point.
Employer NI rates from April 2025: 15% on earnings above £5,000 per employee per year. Companies House fees are current as of 2025. Accountancy fee defaults are national averages and will vary. Not financial or legal advice.
Employer NI 2025/26

From April 2025, employer NI is charged at 15% on earnings above £5,000 per employee per year (down from £9,100).

The Employment Allowance of £10,500 is available to eligible employers to offset their employer NI bill.
