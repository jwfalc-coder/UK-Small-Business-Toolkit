How the Flat Rate Scheme works

Under the FRS, you charge your customers the normal 20% VAT rate. Instead of calculating the exact difference between output and input VAT, you pay HMRC a fixed percentage of your VAT-inclusive (gross) turnover. The percentage depends on your trade sector. The difference between the VAT you collect and the amount you pay HMRC is yours to keep.

The FRS is generally most beneficial for businesses with low input VAT (service businesses with few purchases). Businesses that buy lots of goods or materials often do better under the standard scheme.

Results are estimates for guidance only. Not financial or legal advice. Consult a qualified accountant or HMRC before switching VAT schemes.
Limited cost trader

If your business spends less than 2% of its VAT-inclusive turnover (or less than £1,000 per year) on goods, HMRC classifies you as a limited cost trader. You must use the 16.5% flat rate regardless of your sector. Many IT consultants and contractors are caught by this rule.

First-year discount

If you are in the first year of VAT registration, you get a 1% reduction on your FRS rate. This applies for the first 12 months of VAT registration only.
